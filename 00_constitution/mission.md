# Mission — Broadband Coverage Map of Ukraine

> Status: Draft v1 · Last updated: 2026-05-25

## Pitch

The Broadband Coverage Map of Ukraine is a public, interactive monitoring tool
that maps **fixed-line internet coverage** across every Ukrainian settlement and
tracks the connectivity status of **essential social institutions**. By turning
fragmented coverage data into a single authoritative picture, it drives
data-backed decision-making for government, fosters market transparency for
operators, and enables civic oversight — helping the state meet its digitization
mandate by **2030**.

## The Problem

Today, knowledge of where fixed broadband actually reaches in Ukraine is
fragmented, inconsistent, and largely invisible to the people who need it:

- **Government cannot target investment** without a reliable, settlement-level
  view of where coverage gaps exist and which social institutions remain
  underserved.
- **Social institutions have no benchmark.** A school director, for example,
  cannot easily tell whether their connection is adequate or how it compares to
  neighbouring schools.
- **Operators plan expansion blind.** ISPs lack a shared view of market
  parameters — price and speed by location — to identify viable expansion areas.
- **The public has no oversight tool** to verify that state digitization
  commitments are being met on the ground.

The result is duplicated effort, uneven coverage, and slow progress toward
universal connectivity.

## Who It's For

The map serves three audiences. Government authorities are the primary driver of
design decisions; institution administrators and operators are first-class users
whose needs shape the product.

### 1. Central & Local Government Authorities *(primary)*

Policymakers and administrators who need an authoritative, settlement-level view
of coverage to allocate funding, prioritize underserved areas, hold programs
accountable, and measure progress against the 2030 digitization mandate.

### 2. Social Institution Administrators

Directors and managers of schools, healthcare facilities, and other public
institutions who want to understand their own connectivity status and
**benchmark it against peer institutions** — e.g., a school director comparing
their speed and service against neighbouring schools.

### 3. Internet Service Providers (ISPs)

Operators who monitor the market — **connection parameters such as price and
speed by location** — to identify gaps and plan network expansion, and who
contribute coverage data back into the system.

### Civic / General Public

Citizens and civil society who use the map for transparency and oversight,
verifying that the state is delivering on its connectivity commitments.

## What It Does

- **Map fixed-line coverage** across Ukrainian settlements, showing availability,
  technology, and service parameters (speed, price) at settlement granularity.
- **Track social-institution connectivity**, recording and surfacing the
  connection status of essential public institutions, with peer comparison.
- **Aggregate trustworthy data** from multiple sources into one consistent view:
  - **Operator reporting** — coverage and service data submitted by ISPs.
  - **Government registries** — official settlement and social-institution data.
  - **Crowdsourced / field input** — citizen reports and field validation as
    ground-truth checks.
  - **Open / external data** — third-party and open datasets aggregated in.
- **Expose insights** through interactive maps, comparisons, and decision-support
  views tailored to each audience.

## Differentiators

- **Authoritative, multi-source data.** Combining operator reporting, official
  registries, field validation, and open data makes the picture both
  comprehensive and verifiable — not reliant on any single, gameable source.
- **Settlement-level and institution-level granularity.** Coverage is meaningful
  where decisions are made: per settlement and per social institution.
- **Built for three distinct workflows** — government planning, institutional
  benchmarking, and operator market analysis — rather than a one-size-fits-all
  map.
- **Public and transparent by default**, turning the dataset into an instrument
  of civic accountability.

## Scope

**In scope**

- Fixed-line broadband coverage only (e.g., FTTx, DOCSIS/cable, xDSL).
- Connectivity tracking for essential social institutions.
- Coverage and service parameters: availability, technology, speed, price.
- Geographic granularity at the settlement level.

**Out of scope (initially)**

- Mobile (3G/4G/5G) and satellite (e.g., Starlink) coverage layers.
- Individual end-user account or billing data.
- Real-time network performance/uptime monitoring.

## Vision & Success

By **2028**, the Broadband Coverage Map is the canonical reference for the state
of fixed broadband in Ukraine: government allocates connectivity investment
against it, institutions benchmark and improve their service through it,
operators plan expansion with it, and the public uses it to hold the state
accountable to its digitization mandate. Success means coverage gaps are visible,
shrinking, and demonstrably acted upon.
